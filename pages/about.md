<h1><b>About CLOSER Discovery</b></h1>
<p></p>
<p>CLOSER Discovery is a research tool which enables the research and data community to <b>find</b>, <b>assess</b> and <b>understand</b> UK longitudinal population study data at all stages of the research process.  </p>

<iframe width="561" height="316" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/9mFF4hTPOrI?si=T3wEeCe0c4gd4fx3" title="YouTube video player"></iframe>

<p></p>
<p>Search, browse and explore the data and questionnaires to find:</p>
<ul>
    <li>An overview of each study.  </li>
    <li>Relevant survey questions and variables, which you can filter by Study, Sweep, Topic and Life Stage. </li>
    <li>The questionnaires and questions asked to generate the data, including where questions appeared in the survey and full question flow.  </li>
    <li>The sources of derived variables (but not the derivation methodology). </li>
    <li>Details of each variable, including valid and invalid cases; maximum, minimum and mean values; and in many cases, a frequency count. </li>
    <li>The <a href="/page/scales/10">standardised scales</a> used across studies and waves of data collection.</li>
    <li>A summary of <a href="/page/genetic/5">genetic data</a> collections across the studies with links to how to access the data. </li>


</ul>

<p>Save and share lists of datasets and questionnaires, or specific questions and variables of interest, to a downloadable <a href="/Basket">List</a> for your own reference, research proposals, collaborations, or data requests. You will need to make an <a href="/page/faqs/4#closer-discovery-accounts">account</a> to create a list so it can be saved. </p>


<h2><b>What is the purpose of CLOSER Discovery?</b></h2>

<p>CLOSER Discovery is a FAIR-ready research tool and infrastructure which aims to: </p>
<ul>

<li>Provide the best available information and tools to empower users to find, assess and understand the data, by: </li>
<ul><li>Bringing together the most widely-used UK longitudinal population studies. </li>
<li>Enriching the data to enable researchers to test their robustness. </li>
<li>Creating consistency between different data collections to enable within and cross study comparison. </li>
<li>Enhancing the provenance of the data to support reproducible, transparent and quality research. </li>
</ul>
<li>Encourage innovative data management to improve efficiencies, reduce duplication and build resilient processes.  </li>
<li>Leverage metadata standards to support national and international interoperability and ensure long-term sustainability. </li>

</ul>

<p>If you would like to read more about how CLOSER Discovery aligns with, and implements the FAIR principles, please refer to the <a href="/page/fair/31">FAIR infrastructure page</a>.

</p><h2><b>How do we do this? </b></h2>
<p>CLOSER Discovery is built using metadata. Simply put, metadata is data about data. By using metadata and metadata standards, CLOSER Discovery allows you to find out what is available in 13 of the UK’s longitudinal datasets, make an assessment about whether the data would be useful for you to pursue and understand the context of how the data were created.</p>

<p><a href="https://closer.ac.uk/what-we-do/ " target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">CLOSER’s organisational values</a> are the principles that drive our behaviour and guide our decision making. CLOSER Discovery is guided by these values of collaboration, respect, excellence, sustainability and trust. We work closely with studies and the wider metadata community to ensure: </p>


<ul>

<li>Consistent documentation and controlled vocabularies.  </li>
<li>Transparency of data through the provision of provenance metadata. </li>
<li>Open sharing of knowledge and best practices. </li>
<li>A robust infrastructure that can evolve with research needs. </li>
</ul>

<p>For users who are interested in metadata and the technical aspects of CLOSER Discovery, please visit the <a href="https://wiki.ucl.ac.uk/display/CLOS/What+is+the+CLOSER+Technical+Wiki " target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">CLOSER Technical Wiki</a>.</p>
